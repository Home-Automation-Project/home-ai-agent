# Leftover Management and Repurposing Conventions

## Leftover Classification

### High-Priority Use (Use Next Day)
- **Cooked proteins**: Chicken, beef, pork
  - Reason: Shorter shelf life, good for many dishes
  - Best uses: Salads, sandwiches, wraps, fried rice
  - Freeze if > 2 days
  
- **Prepared sauces**: Tomato sauce, curry, stew base
  - Reason: Good base for new meals
  - Best uses: Pasta, rice, soup, pizza
  - Freeze easily
  
- **Fresh-cooked rice/pasta**: 
  - Reason: High spoilage risk if not used
  - Best uses: Fried rice, pasta bake, soup
  - Freeze well

### Medium-Priority Use (Use within 2-3 days)
- **Cooked vegetables**: [Types that hold well]
  - Best uses: Sides, salads, soups, fried rice
  - Freeze tolerance: Depends on vegetable
  
- **Casseroles and baked dishes**:
  - Best uses: Reheat and serve, or deconstruct for new dish
  - Freeze tolerance: Most freeze well

### Low-Priority Use (Transform or Freeze)
- **Stale bread**:
  - Transform into: Breadcrumbs, croutons, bread pudding, panzanella salad
  - Freeze: Yes (for baking projects)
  
- **Vegetable scraps**:
  - Transform into: Stock, compost, or dried vegetable powder
  - Freeze: Yes (for stock-making later)

## Transformation Recipes

### Cooked Chicken Leftovers
- **Chicken salad**: Mix with mayo, celery, onion, grapes
- **Chicken tacos**: Shred and season with taco seasoning
- **Chicken fried rice**: Dice and stir-fry with rice, vegetables, soy sauce
- **Chicken soup**: Add to broth with vegetables and noodles
- **Chicken quesadillas**: Shred and fill with cheese
- **Chicken pasta**: Toss with pasta, sauce, vegetables

### Cooked Beef Leftovers
- **Beef tacos/burritos**: Shred or dice, season
- **Beef fried rice**: Dice and use like chicken
- **Beef soup**: Add to broth for hearty soup
- **Shepherd's pie**: Use as filling under mashed potatoes
- **Beef hash**: Dice and crisp in a skillet with potatoes
- **Beef sandwich**: Slice thinly for sandwiches

### Cooked Pork Leftovers
- **Pork tacos**: Shred and season
- **Pork fried rice**: Dice and use like chicken
- **Pork soup**: Add to broth or curry base
- **Pork sandwich**: Slice and warm with sauce
- **Pork carbonara**: Dice and use in pasta with egg sauce
- **Pork lo mein**: Add to stir-fried noodles

### Rice Leftovers
- **Fried rice**: Best use; heat oil, add aromatics, vegetables, rice, egg, soy sauce
- **Rice soup**: Add to broth and boil until slightly overcooked
- **Rice pudding**: Sweet version with milk, sugar, cinnamon
- **Arancini**: Form into balls, stuff with cheese/meat, and fry
- **Rice patties**: Mix with egg, form into patties, pan-fry
- **Stuffed peppers**: Use as filling with meat and vegetables

### Pasta Leftovers
- **Pasta bake**: Mix with sauce and cheese, bake until bubbly
- **Pasta soup**: Add to broth with vegetables
- **Frittata/omelet**: Chop and mix into eggs
- **Pasta salad**: Toss with dressing and fresh vegetables (eat cold)
- **Mac and cheese**: Add more cheese sauce and bake

### Vegetable Leftovers

#### Soft Vegetables (cooked zucchini, squash, eggplant)
- Frittata filling
- Pasta sauce ingredient
- Soup addition
- Reheat as side dish

#### Hard Vegetables (carrots, root vegetables)
- Add to soups or stews
- Freeze for later use in stock
- Chop fine and use in fried rice
- Mash if very soft

#### Leafy Vegetables (cooked spinach, kale)
- Pasta filling
- Egg dish ingredient
- Soup addition
- Blend into sauce

### Sauce Leftovers
- **Tomato sauce**: Freeze in portions, use for pasta, pizza, soup, eggs
- **Curry sauce**: Freeze for later use with different protein
- **Gravy**: Freeze for reuse, thin with water if needed
- **Pesto**: Freeze in ice cube trays, use for pasta or sandwiches

## Storage Best Practices

### Container Selection
- **Glass containers**: Best for reheating (microwave safe)
- **Plastic containers**: Good for freezer (label with date)
- **Aluminum foil**: Good for freezer, wrap tightly
- **Freezer bags**: Space-efficient for liquid/sauce items

### Labeling
- **Label format**: Date + Contents + Reheating instructions
- **Example**: "12/15 - Chicken curry - Reheat 350°F 20 min or MW 3 min"
- **Tape used**: Use freezer tape or mark with permanent marker

### Temperature Management
- **Refrigerator storage**: 35-40°F, use within 3-4 days
- **Freezer storage**: 0°F or below, use within [timeframe by item]
- **Thawing**: Move to fridge 24 hours before use (not counter)
